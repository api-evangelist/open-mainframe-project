<div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element ">
	<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We are happy to welcome our new 2026 Open Mainframe Project ambassadors! These are a group of committed people who care deeply about sharing and growing the use of mainframe technology in many industries.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The main goal of the Ambassadors Program is to let more people know how they can join the community and help move Open Mainframe Project forward. The new ambassadors come from different parts of the world and work at companies and groups that use mainframe technology. They were chosen for their skills, leadership, and strong support for the Open Mainframe Project’s goals.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">These ambassadors have offered to give talks, guide others, look over materials or slides, and write blogs or articles that highlight the Open Mainframe Foundation and its many projects.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We are excited to have this group of people with different skills, experiences, and technical knowledge help grow the Open Mainframe Project around the world.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Here, you will find each ambassador’s personal bio, three fun facts they have shared, and their reason for wanting to be part of the ambassador program.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">To see last year’s group, check out the </span><a href="https://openmainframeproject.org/ambassadors/meet-the-2025-open-mainframe-project-ambassadors/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">2025 Open Mainframe Project Ambassadors blog</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<h2><b>Bruno Laurent</b></h2>
<h2><b><img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-16814 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="https://openmainframeproject.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/14/2026/03/redgoldphoenix-150x150.jpg" width="150" /></b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">With over 25 years of mainframe expertise since 1998, Bruno is passionate about bridging traditional mainframe environments with modern cloud-native technologies. As an Endevor Administrator at Euroclear, I lead modernization initiatives that transform how development teams work with COBOL applications, implementing cutting-edge DevOps practices on z/OS.</span></p>
<p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“I believe the mainframe&#8217;s future lies in open-source collaboration and modern tooling. Technologies like Zowe, GitHub Actions, and containerization are not threats to the mainframe—they&#8217;re opportunities to attract new talent and demonstrate that z/OS is a platform for innovation. I&#8217;m committed to mentoring the next generation, sharing real-world modernization experiences, and proving that mainframes remain relevant in today&#8217;s cloud-first world.”</span></i></p>
<p><b>Three fun facts about Bruno:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b> 27 Years and Counting </b> <span style="font-weight: 400;">I&#8217;ve been in the mainframe world since 1998. That&#8217;s longer than some of my colleagues have been alive! I started as an operator when people thought mainframes were &#8220;legacy.&#8221; Today, I&#8217;m proving they&#8217;re cutting-edge by running Zowe, integrating with Azure DevOps, and speaking at international conferences. The mainframe didn&#8217;t retire; it just got a DevOps degree!</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b> Dijon: Not Just Mustard! </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">I live in Dijon, France, yes, the mustard capital! While most people associate my city with condiments, I spend my time cooking up COBOL CI/CD pipelines and mainframe modernization recipes. My secret ingredient? A blend of 27-year-old mainframe expertise with fresh cloud-native technologies. Much tastier than mustard, I promise!</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b> From Hair to There </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">I grew my hair long for several years and donated it to associations that create wigs for cancer patients. </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Because giving back to the community isn&#8217;t just about sharing code and knowledge. Sometimes it&#8217;s about sharing what you can grow! (And yes, my colleagues were quite surprised when I showed up with short hair one day!)</span></li>
</ul>
<h2><b>Richelle Anne Craw</b></h2>
<h2><b><img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-16815 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="https://openmainframeproject.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/14/2026/03/richellecraw-150x150.jpg" width="150" /></b></h2>

I enjoy mentoring and teaching newcomers about Zowe, supporting colleagues learning modern tools, and also sharing real stories about women in mainframe via the Mainframe Coven.”</span></p>

<h2><b><img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-16813 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="https://openmainframeproject.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/14/2026/03/selfnewpic1-Saurabh-Banerjee-150x150.jpg" width="150" /></b></h2>

<p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">“As an Ambassador, I want to give back to the community by amplifying OMP initiatives, contributing practical insights from real enterprise modernization programs, and helping make the mainframe more accessible, relevant, and future-ready for both experienced professionals and newcomers alike.”</span></i></p>

<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Formula 1 Enthusiast – I’m a big F1 fan and rarely miss a race. The precision, strategy, and engineering behind Formula 1 racing strongly resonate with how I think about building high-performance technology systems.</span></li>

<h2><b>Victor Taiwo</b></h2>
<p><img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-16812 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="https://openmainframeproject.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/14/2026/03/IMG_3222-Victor-Taiwo-150x150.jpg" width="150" /></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Victor Taiwo is a Computer Science undergraduate at Northern Illinois University and an IBM Z Student Ambassador specializing in mainframe development and AI-mainframe integration. He is the Founder and Lead Developer of Zcrafter, an AI-powered mainframe terminal emulator built on Open Mainframe Project&#8217;s Zowe that helps developers work with COBOL, JCL, and mainframe systems using natural language processing. Victor has extensive experience with z/OS administration, COBOL, JCL, Assembler, and RACF security. He is passionate about modernizing mainframe technology and bridging traditional 3270 interfaces with modern AI capabilities to improve developer efficiency and usability. Through the IBM Z Student Ambassador Program, Victor has organized campus workshops, enabling over 20 students to earn IBM Z certifications, and actively advocates for mainframe technology awareness.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“I want to be an Open Mainframe Project Ambassador because I am passionate about bridging the gap between mainframe technology and modern AI capabilities. Through my work on Zcrafter, built on Open Mainframe Project&#8217;s Zowe, I have experienced firsthand how open source mainframe projects can transform developer experiences and drive innovation in the mainframe ecosystem.”</span></p>
